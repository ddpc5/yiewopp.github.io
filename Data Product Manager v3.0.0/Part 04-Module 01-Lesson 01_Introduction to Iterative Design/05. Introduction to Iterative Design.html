Summary

Cycle of iterative design

The cycle of iterative design starts with designing something. It may be a product or a feature for a product. After the feature is designed, it can be put in place, or implemented. Once the new design has been put in front of others, it is time to evaluate the performance of the design. As evaluation suggests ways that a design can be made stronger, we return to the design stage and the cycle of iterative design continues.

Designing thinking cycle

The design thinking cycle expands on the cycle of iterative design by laying out three stages and six steps that highlight data-driven best practices for changing or creating features and products.

The first stage in the cycle is Understand. This includes
empathizing users' needs by conducting research and defining users' problems by combining all research results.

After the Understand stage comes the Explore stage, where different ideas are explored to meet users' needs. The first step in this stage is ideation when product managers and designers think broadly and generate a large list of potential ideas. Once many ideas have been generated, a few that seem like the best fit could be prototyped.

Then the design thinking cycle enters the Materialize stage. The first step in this stage is testing the change. It includes getting feedback and running experiments on prototypes. After testing, the new design is implemented for all users.

With the new design launched, a product manager will continue to analyze data and reach out to customers to identify areas that could be improved. With this, the cycle begins again. By following this cycle, product managers leverage data to drive the creation of the most appealing product for users.
