User Conversion Funnels Exercise Solution: Online Shopping Experience

ND030 C3 L2 06 User Aquisition And Activation Funnels Exercise Solution Video

Summary

While the specific user acquisition and activation funnel you followed when making an online purchase will vary, the below covers the main stages and the most common experiences of an e-commerce funnel:

The funnel begins:

1) The customer has a need for a product. This can come from external triggers or via advertising (paid marketing).

Example: sick of her dog's chew toys being pulled apart, potential customer Nina decides to look for more rugged options

Awareness:

2) The customer learns about the website, perhaps while searching for a particular type of item. Paid advertising can also come into play here via Adwords and other marketing techniques.

Example: when Nina searches on Google for "toys for dog chewy" she sees an ad for DogToys.com

3) The customer enters the website

Example: Nina clicks on the DogToys.com ad. Because of the "chewy" word in her search, the link drops her in the "extra tough" section.

Evaluation:

4) The customer searches for potential items to purchase

Example: Nina looks at the reviews on the three top-rated extra-tough dog toys

5) The customer decides to make a purchase

Example: Nina places two dog toys in her cart and chooses to continue to checkout.

Purchase

6) The customer follows through on their intent and makes a purchase.

Example: Nina puts in her shipping address and credit card information and hits submit. Fido gets new toys and Nina has completed this part of the funnel!

Retention & Reactivation

Of course, online shops don't want customers to only make one purchase. They'll use lots of techniques to keep you interested and try to attract you back. Perhaps the delivery box contains a coupon for 15% off your next purchase. If you provided an email when purchasing the item, you'll likely receive targeted marketing emails as well.
