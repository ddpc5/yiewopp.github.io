Funnel analysis

Creating a user funnel starts with identifying the steps a user takes when going towards the desired action, such as making a purchase or using a new feature.

Then product managers count the number of users who take each step. The shape of a funnel occurs because the number of users is reduced as customers “fall out”

Lastly, product managers look for where large drop off occurs. The steps where the large drop off occurs indicates areas that may be confusing or difficult for customers to make.

Traditional sales funnel

A conversion funnel can be looked at in three stages. ** awareness, evaluation, and purchase.** These funnels allow us to dig into detailed data about a customer’s step by step journey to find specific areas for improvement.

Awareness

Awareness is when the user becomes aware of a need and/or a product. Users in the awareness stage discover the product based on their needs.

Evaluation

Evaluation is when the user researches their options. Users in the evaluation stage take time to learn about their purchase options and consider whether a purchase is aligned with their interests.

Purchase

Purchase is when the user takes the desired action such as becoming a paying customer. This is the final stage because, in this stage, the ultimate goal is met.

Users can take many steps in each stage. By looking at the number of users in each step, you can determine where the largest drop off occurs and decide where to investigate further.

Example: eCommerce purchase funnel

Example: eCommerce purchase funnel

During the awareness stage, the steps can be:

  • Advertisement viewing
  • Site visit

Then, the user moves to the evaluation stage. Steps in this stage may include:

  • Review the product and consider a purchase
  • Add items to cart
  • Enter checkout page

The final stage is the purchase stage. The steps in this stage may be:

  • Make a purchase

Funnel analysis in feature usage

Funnel analysis in feature usage is almost identical to that in the product level. It starts with identifying the steps a user takes to complete the desired actions. In the case of feature usage, the desired action may not be making a purchase, but use of the new feature.

Regardless of whether the goal is purchase or usage, the analysis still consists of counting the number of users who make it to each step and looking at areas with a high drop off which indicate a need for further investigation and potential improvement.

New terms

  • Awareness: The step in a sales funnel when the user becomes aware of a need and/or a product.
  • Evaluation: The step in a sales funnel when the user researches their options and considers whether a product will bring them value.
  • Purchase: The step in a sales funnel when the user takes the final/desired action of becoming a paying customer
