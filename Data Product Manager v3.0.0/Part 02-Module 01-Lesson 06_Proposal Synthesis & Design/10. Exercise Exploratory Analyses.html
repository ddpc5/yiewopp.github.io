10. Exercise: Exploratory Analyses

Exploratory Analyses Exercise Instructions

Exploratory data analysis is where you start with one general question you have around a dataset, where the answer leads to another question, where the answer leads to even another question, and so forth. You end up with an insight (or multiple) that you and your team can now take action on.

Let’s say you’re the product manager for an e-commerce eco-sustainable shoe store. You designed and ran an A/B test where 50% of customers saw the site as normal and did not receive free shipping (the control group), while the other 50% saw a free shipping promotional banner and automatically received free shipping if their order’s subtotal was greater than or equal to $80.

  • The experiment resulted in 75,000 unique visitors to the control bucket and 75,000 unique visitors to the variant bucket.
  • Visitors were allocated to one of the buckets at random. You can assume that all visitors were new visitors.
  • Using exploratory analysis, determine and present whether or not we should offer free shipping to all customers, leveraging the experiment results, downloadable here.
  • Upload into Tableau or use the below Postgres instance to run the analysis.
