14. Exercise: Box Plots & Histograms

In this exercise, let’s say you’re once again the product manager for a company developing smartwatches, and you’re working on improving the customer experience.

You’re looking to conduct some user research to bolster up the internationalization of your product. You want to speak with users from countries with high average ratings of your smartwatches, to understand what is going well, and also with users from countries with low average ratings of your product, to learn about what could be improved.

You can find the reviews dataset here. Remember, smartwatches have a product id of 3. For this exercise, you can either upload the data to Tableau immediately or use the below SQL workspace to get started analyzing the data.

Code

If you need a code on the https://github.com/udacity.

Visualization type

You want to determine which countries have the highest and lowest average ratings of your smartwatches. What type of visualization should you use?
SOLUTION: A bar chart showing average rating by country

Highest ratings

You decide to start with the positives, and identify the five countries with the highest average ratings. Which five countries do you select?
SOLUTION: China, Mexico, Russia, Brazil, Estonia

Histogram

Ratings are on a 5-point scale, but you decide you want to simplify this into 3 categories: ratings of 1-2 as Low, ratings of 3-4 as Mid, and ratings of 5 as High. How would you do this?
SOLUTION: A histogram with buckets of size 2.1
