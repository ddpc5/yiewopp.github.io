ND030 C1 L2 A08 Visualizations - Box Plots & Histograms - Exercise Solution

Visualizations: Box Plots & Histograms Exercise Solution Summary

Make your Review measure into a row, and cast it as an average. Then, again filter your dataset on a Product ID of 3 for smartwatches. Then, make the Country dimension a column, to see the highest average reviews by country.

To simplify the review scale, remove Country as a column, then cast the Review measure in “Rows” as a count of values. Then, select histogram as the chart type. Make the bin size 2.1, so that there are 3 bins in the histogram (values 1-5 split up every 2.1 values will make 3 bins).

Why bins of size 2.1 instead of 2?

Recall that we want three bins: a bin for reviews of 1-2, and bin for reviews of 3-4, and a bin for reviews of 5. Your instinct might be to set the bin sizes to 2, but if you try this in Tableau you can see that the first bin will be created up to but not including 2, the second bin will start at 2 and go up to but not including 4, and the final bin will start at 4. This results in reviews of 1 having their own bin, and reviews of 3-4 and 4-5 being grouped together. By setting the bin size to 2.1, we can ensure that reviews of 2 will be sorted into the first bin, reviews of 3-4 into the second bin, and the third bin will contain only reviews of 5.
