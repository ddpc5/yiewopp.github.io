Internal Stakeholders You’ll Work With as a Data Product Manager

ND030 C1 L0 A05 Business Stakeholders

Business Stakeholders Summary

How data product managers work with different internal stakeholders throughout the company:

Data science - To help you understand the nuances in the data, to help determine success metrics of your product, to run any deep statistical analyses, to build ML algorithms and models (if they are ML-focused).

Design - To create near-seamless physical and digital interactions and pixel perfect placement to empower customers to use and love the product

Product marketing - To help determine pricing, strategize on market positioning, assess market viability, establish product/brand voice, estimate the total addressable market, create a rollout strategy, and produce communications collateral.

User research - recruit and interview users from different customer segments to provide context on the viability of what we’re building actually solved user problems, before & after launch.

Data engineering - to collect, transform and store batch and/or streaming data into the desired output & location for analyses.

Client engineering (Web, Android, iOS) - to build intuitive interfaces that display data for end user consumption, also collects event data (or user actions).

Server engineering - Also known as backend or platform engineering in some contexts. To build resilient and accessible databases, data stores, and caching layers for frontend applications to be able to successfully create, read, update, and delete data on a real-time (or dependent on your product requirements). Receives and stores event data from frontend clients, to later send off to the data warehouse.

Legal, privacy, and compliance teams - to determine data processing regulation requirements, and/or ensure that the data products you are building are compliant with consumer protection laws.

Finance & business operations - to help you measure unit economics, business growth, and financial health metrics (like revenue, churn, retention, gross margins, new user acquisition rates, profit, loss), or quantify opportunities for regional & global expansion

Sales & customer success - As the product manager, you’ll need to evangelize the limitations & capabilities of the current product, in addition to providing them insights on the future roadmap. They’ll also provide you feedback on the reasons or lack of features on why potential customers may not end up signing deals or going to other competitors.

Localization/Internationalization - to have your product localized, or have text dynamically translated into the various languages you support for international adoption and accessibility.

Quality assurance engineering - their goal is to break your product, or expose bugs in your product before your external users do. They may end up finding a lot more issues than your team can physically work on and you’ll need to leverage analytics to make a determination on which issues to tackle first based on existing feature usage.
