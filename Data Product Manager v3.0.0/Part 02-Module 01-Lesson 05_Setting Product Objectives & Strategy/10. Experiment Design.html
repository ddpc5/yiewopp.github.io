10. Experiment Design

Experiment Design Heading

Experiment Design

ND030 C1 L3 A04.1 Experiment Design

An experiment is a test whose primary function is to validate a hypothesis, whereas a hypothesis is a proposed statement that requires validation. Experiments are a good way to validate how a feature might affect KPIs without launching the feature 100% and having to roll it back if it turns out to be unsuccessful.

ND030 C1 L3 A06.2A Experiment Design

ND030 C1 L3 A06.2B Experiment Design

ND030 C1 L3 A06.2C Experiment Design

The results of an experiment are considered to be statistically significant when the likelihood that the results are due to random chance is below a certain threshold (called a p-value). In order to achieve statistically significant results, it is important to have a large enough group of participants in your experiment. The number of participants needed for the experiment to produce statistically significant results is called the sample size.
