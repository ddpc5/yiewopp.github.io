15. Solution: Ensure Unbiased Control & Test Groups Exercise

Solution: Ensure Unbiased Control & Test Groups Exercise

ND030 C3 L5 10 Ensuring Unbiased Control & Test Groups Exercise Solution Video

Summary

Scenario 1 :

Since the business is working with existing users, they should be able to easily create an unbiased test and control group based on the information they have on their users.

First, they should randomly assign users to either the test or control condition. It would be most helpful if a user in one group sees either "A" or "B" no matter how or where they log in. They should also check to ensure the user characteristics remain relatively balanced between control and test groups.

By using random assignment, it is likely that devices, operating systems/browsers, and even user types (newer vs. established users; frequent vs. infrequent users) would be relatively similar across both groups. However, the business should confirm that these user characteristics are roughly similar across both groups to prevent unintentional bias.

Scenario 2:

The business should randomly place new visitors into control and test groups. They can help ensure the visitors are truly new by dropping a cookie on all visitors and only showing putting users into an experiment or control group if they do not yet have a visitor's cookie. As they are performing a multivariate test, the business may want to use a framework for multivariate testing that assigns users at different rates based on the impact seen of different designs.

As business A, if they randomly assign users, they should confirm that user characteristics that might impact the effect of the experiments are balanced among the control and experiment groups. For example, the business should ensure that each experiment has a balanced number of users on different devices/OS/browsers. Since the business is testing designs with first-time users, they won't have much more information about the users to differentiate upon. If they randomly assign users, other user characteristics that might impact the effect of the experiments should balance out among the control and experiment groups.
