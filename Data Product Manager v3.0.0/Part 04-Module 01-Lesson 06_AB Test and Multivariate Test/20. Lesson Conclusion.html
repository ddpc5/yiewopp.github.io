Congratulations! You have just finished the sixth and final lesson.

Glossary

  • A/B test: An experiment to seeing if a single change has an effect compared to what is currently in place.
  • Alternative hypothesis: Hypothesis in testing that is contrary to the null hypothesis. Usually the alternative hypothesis is that any differences observed are due to a real effect of the experiment.
  • Control group: The group of users in an experiment for whom the user experience is not changed.
  • Conversion rate: For an experiment, the conversion rate is the percentage of users who performed the desired action out of all users in a test group.
  • Multivariate test: An experiment to see the effects of a changes of multiple variables, including how the variables interact with each other.
  • Null hypothesis: Hypothesis in testing that there is no difference between test groups, and that any differences observed are due to error or random chance.
  • P value: The value that indicates the probability of obtaining results as extreme as the observed results of a statistical hypothesis test, assuming that the null hypothesis is correct. A smaller p-value means the evidence in favor of the alternative hypothesis is stronger.
  • Test/experiment group: Group or groups of users for whom the user experience is changed in some way during an experiment.
  • T-Test: A type of statistical analysis that compares the means of two groups to see whether the difference in the means is large enough that it is probably not due to random chance.
