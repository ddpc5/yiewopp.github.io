Security and Compliance

One of the most important aspects to keep in mind when building a data strategy is Data Security. There are many regulations and standards in today's world that have to be incorporated into a Data Strategy. If these are not addressed, your company will be open to huge fines for violations. Below you will find most of the critical items to consider, but remember that one of our stakeholders, Security and/or Legal, will help address compliance regulation.

PII

PII: "Personal data, also known as personal information or personally identifiable information (PII)is any information relating to an identifiable person."

Keep the following in mind when Handling PII:

  • Know local laws and definitions
  • Identify where all PII is
    • Data at rest: Data that is stored in a storage device and is not actively used
    • Data in motion: Data that is being used
  • Establish data retention policies
  • Governance and controls

PCI DSS

PCI DSS: "The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) is an information security standard for organizations that handle branded credit cards from the major card schemes."

If you have PCI data, you need to follow stringent compliance guidelines, and there will be regular audits. If the procedures are not followed, there will be fines and legal ramifications.

Core Control Objectives of PCI Data

  1. Build and Maintain a Secure Network and Systems
  2. Protect Cardholder Data
  3. Maintain a Vulnerability Management Program
  4. Implement Strong Access Control Measures
  5. Regularly Monitor and Test Networks
  6. Maintain an Information Security Policy

HIPAA

HIPAA- Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
**Protected Health Information (PHI)- medical record numbers, device identifiers and serial numbers. Any company or individual that comes into contact with PHI must enact and enforce appropriate policies, procedures, and safeguards to protect data.

Violations lead to:

  • Termination from Job
  • Sanctions from professional boards
  • Criminal charges including fines and imprisonment

More information: HIPAA for Dummies

GDPR

GDPR: General Data Protection Regulation

  • European Union and European Economic Area
  • More control to citizens over their data
  • Fines up to 20 Million Euros or 4% of global turnover
  • Know what you have and why you have it?
  • Where is the data, who uses it, and how is it used?
  • Encryption is key
  • Data Governance is highly recommended

CCPA

CCPA: California Consumer Privacy Act

This act gives Californians the right

  • To know what personal information is being collected about them?
  • To Know whether their personal information is sold or disclosed and to whom?
  • To say no to the sale of personal information.
  • To access their personal data.
  • To have equal service and price, even if they exercise their privacy rights.
