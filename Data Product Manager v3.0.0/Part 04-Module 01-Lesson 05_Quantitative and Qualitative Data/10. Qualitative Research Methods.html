10. Qualitative Research Methods

Qualitative Research Methods

ND030 C3 L4 07 Qualitative Research Methods Video

Summary

There are three popular qualitative research methods used by product managers today: focus groups, interviews, and usability testing.

Focus group

A focus group is a group of participants from the target market gathered together in one room with a moderator. The moderator leads the group in a discussion of topics around a product or feature.

  • Benefits
    • Time-saving: 6-10 people provide opinions at once
  • Drawbacks
    • Limited time to dig into an individual participant’s background
    • Participants can be influenced by the opinions of others

User interview.

A user interview is a one-on-one discussion with a customer or potential customer. The interview script may focus on the user journey or seek opinions on a potential new feature.

  • Benefits

    • Ability to dig deeply into interviewee’s background
    • Interviewee not influenced by others (vs. focus groups)

  • Drawbacks

    • Time-consuming (one person at a time)

Usability testing

Usability testing is when a user or potential user is asked to actually click through a product or prototype to accomplish a task. The usability tester may be asked to “think out loud” and explain why they are doing certain things and what elements confuse them as they try to reach their assigned goal.

  • Benefits
    • Get feedback on actual designs
    • See behavior and usability issues in real-time
  • Drawbacks
    • Only helpful once a prototype exists.

New terms

  • Focus group: A group of people gathered together to have a conversation facilitated by a moderator. The moderator leads the group in a discussion of topics around a product, feature, or other items of interest.
  • Usability testing: A method of product research where a user or potential user is asked to click through a product or prototype to accomplish a task while being observed.
  • User interview: A research method where a one-on-one discussion with a customer or potential customer takes place.
