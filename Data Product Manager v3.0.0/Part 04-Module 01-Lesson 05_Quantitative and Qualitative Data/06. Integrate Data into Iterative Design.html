06. Integrate Data into Iterative Design

Integrate Data into Iterative Design

ND030 C3 L4 05 Integrating Qualitative Data Into The Iterative Design Process Video

Summary

Cycle of quantitative and qualitative data usage

First, analyzing usage data, conversion funnels, and other quantitative data collected can reveal users or areas that are under- and over- performing.

Next, upon seeing areas for improvement via quantitative analysis, product managers then turn to user interviews or other qualitative data techniques to understand why such trends might be occurring.

By combining quantitative and qualitative data, a product manager can form hypotheses of why a trend occurs and what change can address it. Then the product manager can then run experiments to confirm the hypothesis. As the product manager reviews the quantitative data associated with the experiment, the cycle re-starts.

Data in the Design Thinking cycle

Throughout the cycle, product managers leverage both qualitative and quantitative data to drive the creation of the most appealing product for users.

During the Understand stage, and especially in the empathize step, qualitative research is employed to understand a user’s needs. When defining a problem, both qualitative and quantitative data are considered to narrow down the problems that need to be solved.

In the Explore stage where different ideas for meeting a user’s needs are explored, the first step is Ideation. Once many ideas have been generated, a few that seem like the best fit will be prototyped.

Then it enters the Materialize stage. The first step in the Materialize stage is to test the prototype. The test step includes obtaining qualitative data such as feedback on the prototype from users and obtaining quantitative data from the prototype instrumentation. The testing step also includes running experiments (hypothesis test) by exposing some live users to the prototype. Product managers then use the quantitative data or results from the experiment to confirm the effect of the new feature on the product KPIs.

Finally, after testing, the new design is implemented for all users. With the new design launched, a product manager can continue to analyze quantitative data to find trends or areas that are under- and over- performing. With this, the cycle begins again.
