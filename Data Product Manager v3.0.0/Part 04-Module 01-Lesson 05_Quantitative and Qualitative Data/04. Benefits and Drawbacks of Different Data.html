04. Benefits and Drawbacks of Different Data

Benefits and Drawbacks of Different Data

ND030 C3 L4 04 The Benefits And Drawbacks Of Quantitative And Qualitative Data Video

Summary

Quantitative data

Quantitative data is any data the expresses a count, amount, or range in a numeric fashion. Quantitative data can be used to answer questions like How many? How often? and how much?

  • Benefits

    • Provides descriptive measurements of how a product is used
    • Allows the analysis of trends and differences between groups

  • Limitations

    • Can’t tell why a user takes an action
    • There can be many interpretations of the results

Qualitative data

Qualitative data often focuses on human behavior and needs that can’t simply be quantified by measuring user actions. Qualitative data is usually collected by talking to users or potential users, often in interviews or focus groups.

  • Benefits
    • Provides insight into why a user has done something or might be interested in something
    • Gives access to a richer understanding of users' desires and concerns
  • Limitations
    • Collecting and analyzing qualitative data can be very time-consuming
    • Larger themes around customer needs and desires are difficult to quantify
    • Has an inherent risk of inaccuracy

New terms

  • Qualitative: Qualitative data is data . Qualitative data is descriptive data that is observed but typically can't be measured.
  • Quantitative: Quantitative data is any data the expresses a count, amount, or range in a numeric fashion.
