12. Solution: Identify and Calculate KPIs in Customers' Journey

Identify and Calculate KPIs in Customers' Journey Solution

ND030 C3 L1 12 Calculating User AARR Exercise Solution Video

Summary

1) Using the provided data, calculate the basic average customer acquisition cost per month.

CAC = Total acquisition expense / Number of new customers acquired

To calculate the basic CAC, sum all marketing and sales costs put towards acquiring users. Since the BestAppEver allows users access to some of the app's features for free, include the cost of all work done to maintain the free version of the app in this sum. Finally, divide the calculated number (total expense) by the number of new (not returning) users in a given month.

The calculations should range from a low of $91.27 CAC in November to a high of $150.45 in March.

2) The app creator tells you they've been trying different marketing channels every month. Based on the provided data, is there a particular month's method you would suggest she invest in? Is there a particular month's method you suggest she not use again?

The month with the lowest customer acquisition costs would indicate the marketing methods that might be worthy of reuse. For this reason, you should recommend the marketing methods used in November (with the lowest CAC, of $91.27) be further explored.

Since many months had similarly low CACs, the app owner should also consider if all marketing methods brought in the same type of customers. For example, a month with a slightly higher CAC that resulted in higher spending, longer-retained app users might be worth the slightly higher expense. However, if all customers acquired across the months had similar usage and spending behavior, November's marketing methods would likely be the most cost-efficient marketing solution.

The month with the highest customer acquisition costs would indicate the marketing methods that might not be worthy of reuse. For this reason, you should recommend the marketing methods used in March (with the highest CAC, of $150.45) NOT be further explored. Given that the March CAC was far higher than other months, the marketing methods employed during that month are likely not as cost-effective as others.

3) Using the provided data, calculate the lifetime value of an average paying user of the BestAppEver.

To calculate basic lifetime value for an average customer, we multiply the average customer spending (in a given time period) by the average customer lifespan (in the same time units). 

LTV = Average amount spent within a time period X Retention time period

Make sure that the spending and lifespan period use the same time units, such as weeks or months. In the case of the BestAppEver, the average customer lifespan is stated as 12 weeks, so LTV can be calculated by average customer spending in a week * 12 weeks.

The provided data gives a sample of 5 different user purchases over a week who we are told had a level of spending similar to an average customer. To get the average amount spent per user within a week, we add up the weekly spent by each user then divide it by the total number of users. This will result in a value of $23.30.

Once you have established the average spending per week per customer at $23.30, multiply that number by the average customer lifetime (12 weeks for the BestAppEver) to get a customer lifetime value. For the BestAppEver, the average lifetime value of a customer is $279.60.

4) Given the LTV and CACs calculated, is the BestAppEver creator overspending on marketing when trying to acquire new users?

Since the LTV of a customer doesn't take into account the expenses it took to recruit the customer, it is important CAC be (far) less than than the LTV of a customer. Right now, BestAppEver is spending about half of the LTV of the customer ($150 CAC vs. $279.60 LTV) to acquire new customers. This means they aren't losing money from their marketing efforts. Further data about the cost of app maintenance, the size of the addressable market, etc. would be needed to know if the BestAppEver has a solid business plan. However, you can confidently say their customer acquisition costs don't cost more than an average user will return in revenue.
