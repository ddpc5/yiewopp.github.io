05. KPIs for Different Business Models

KPIs for Different Business Models

ND030 C3 L1 05 KPIs For Different Business Models Video

In this section, we discussed three common business models: eCommerce, freemium, and software-as-a-service (SAAS).

eCommerce

eCommerce business model is the traditional model of the sale of an item in exchange for payment where the storefront is online. Some examples are Amazon’s online retail store and Target.com.

Common KPIs for the eCommerce business model include:

  • The profit margin made on sales (payment - cost)
  • The number purchases
  • The number of repeat customers

Software as a service (SAAS)

The software as a service (SAAS) business model offers customers access to software for a period of time in exchange for payment. Users have access to the software for as long as they are paying for the service. Some examples are Microsoft word and Adobe photoshop.

Common KPIs for software as a service (SAAS) business model are:

  • The number of paying users
  • The subscribe renewal rate
  • The percent of users upgrade if the service includes a free trial and allow upgrades

The above KPIs track revenue and potential revenue directly. There are KPIs that do not measure revenue but they are leading indicators of product success. These are:

  • User satisfaction
  • The number of customer support tickets/complaints

Freemium

The freemium business model allows users to use the basic features of service for free, but charges for upgrades. Some examples are Slack, a business communication tool, and Spotify, a music playing service.

Common KPIs for a freemium business model concentrate on those users who pay for the service:

  • The number of paying subscribers
  • The percent of free subscribers who choose to upgrade (conversion rate)
  • Renewal rate

Just like a SAAS product, leading indicators are also important to track. These are:

  • Paid customer satisfaction
  • The number of paid customer support tickets/complaints

Summary

The eCommerce, SAAS, and freemium are the most common business models that modern product managers see. When choosing KPIs, it is important to consider how the product generates revenue based on its business model.

The KPIs can take two forms:

  • directly impacts revenue
  • leading indicators that help to predict revenue-related metrics

New terms

  • eCommerce: The traditional business model of the sale of an item in exchange for payment. For eCommerce, the storefront is online.
  • Freemium: Business model that allows users to use the basic features of service for free, but charges for upgrades or the removal of advertisements.
  • Leading Indicators: Any metric that changes before the rest of a product's metrics begin to go in a particular direction. Leading indicators help product managers predict whether a product's revenue is on the path to success.
  • Software as a Service (SAAS): Business model that offers customers access to software in exchange for payment. Typically users have access to the software for as long as they are paying for the service.
