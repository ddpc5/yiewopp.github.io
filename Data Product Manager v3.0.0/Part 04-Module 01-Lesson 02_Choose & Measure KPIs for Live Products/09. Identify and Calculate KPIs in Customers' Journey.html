09. Identify and Calculate KPIs in Customers' Journey

Identify and Calculate KPIs in Customers' Journey

Customers' Journey: Acquisition and Activation

ND030 C3 L1 08 Calculating User AARR Video Part 1

Summary

Product managers need to look for ways to improve products and to drive further revenue and customer satisfaction. By looking at KPIs related to customer’s journey, product managers can learn where to improve and develop.

The customer's journey can be broken down into Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, and Referral categories. This set of metrics is called the Pirate Metrics.

Acquisition

Acquisition is about acquiring new visitors. It is the first step in the Pirate Metrics because being aware of the existence of the product is the first step in a user's journey.

KPIs around user acquisition focus on how many and how often users can find your product. Some examples are

  • The number of new visitors in a day
  • The number of new visitors coming from different sources

If KPIs in this category don't perform well, a product manager needs to do more work to help customers find the product.

Activation

Activation is how quickly new visitors find the product valuable and decide to become a customer. It is the second step in the pirate metrics framework because new visitors become customers/users before they continue their journey.

Activation KPIs focus on whether someone goes from being aware of your product to becoming a customer. This is also called activation conversion. Some example activation KPIs are:

  • The time users spend browsing the product on the first visit
  • The number of visitors who sign up for an account

If the activation KPIs aren’t performing well, a product manager needs to make the product more appealing or simplify the onboarding/signup process.

Customers' Journey: Retention and Revenue

Retention

Retention measures how often or how many users come back. It is the third step in the Pirate Metrics framework. A product still won’t be a successful one if it doesn't retain or keep active users. It is often expensive to find new customers, so successful products retain users for as long as possible.

Retention KPIs focus on the number of users who return and continue using your product. Some example retention KPIs are:

  • The number of customers who renew their subscriptions
  • The average time length a user remains a customer.

If retention KPIs don't perform well, a product manager needs to do more work to keep users, probably by providing features that meet customer needs.

Revenue

Revenue measures the amount of money a product makes. It
is the fourth step in the Pirate Metrics framework.

Besides revenue and profit, there are two more revenue KPIs that are important:

  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). It is how much you spend on acquiring paying customers. The lower this cost is, the higher the profit could be.
  • Customer lifetime value (LTV). This is the amount of revenue an average customer generates. Higher LTV indicates greater revenue that is generated from each customer on average. Note: a specific customer may have a higher or lower lifetime value.

By comparing CAC to LTV, you can see if the product is profitable. If LTV is less than CAC, the product cannot be profitable.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).

It is how much you spend on acquiring paying customers.

CAC = TE / NC

CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost
TE: Total expense on customer acquisition
NC: Number of new customers acquired

Customer lifetime value (LTV)

This is the amount of revenue an average customer generates.

LTV = amount_per_user * RT
    = value_per_sale * number_transaction * RT

LTV: Lifetime value
RT: Retention time period
amount_per_user: Average amount spent per user within a time period 
value_per_sale: Average value of a sale
number_transaction: Average number of transactions within a time period

New terms

  • Acquisition: User acquisition metrics measure the number of users who learn about a product or feature.
  • Activation: User activation metrics measure how quickly new visitors decide to become active users and/or paying customers.
  • Activation Conversion: When a person goes from being aware of your product to actively using and/or paying for the product.
  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): How much is spent on acquiring paying customers, per customer acquired.
  • Customer lifetime value (LTV): The amount of revenue an average customer generates.
  • **Pirate Metrics **: A set of metrics to help predict whether a product is on the path to success. Created by Doug McLure in 2007, the pirate metric categories are awareness, acquisition, activation, revenue, retention and referral (AARRR).
  • Referral:: Referral metrics measure how often and how well current users are referring new users to your product.
  • Retention:Retention metrics measure how long, on average, users remain active users and/or paying customers.
  • Revenue:: Revenue metrics measure the revenue (or money earned) by a product or feature.
