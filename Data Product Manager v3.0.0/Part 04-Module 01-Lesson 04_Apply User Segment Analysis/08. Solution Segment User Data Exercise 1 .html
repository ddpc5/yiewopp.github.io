08. Solution: Segment User Data Exercise 1

Solution: Food Delivery App User Segments

ND030 C3 L3 07 Segmenting Data By User Cohorts Exercise Solution Video

Summary

There are innumerable behavioral and user characteristics that might impact how a customer uses a food delivery app. However, some characteristics seem more likely than others. Three examples are provided below.

  1. Consider the users of a food delivery app. Generate a list of 3 behavioral or user characteristics that might relate to differences in the way users orders food via that app.
  • Age
  • Device
  • Location
  1. Provide a reason that might impact the way a user orders food via a food delivery app.
  • Age: Teenage & college-aged students might order food at different times of day than older adults who are more likely to be working typical 9-5 hours. For example, younger users might order more late at night whereas older users might order more in the 6-8pm dinner window.
  • Device: Different devices provide different layouts and options. Tablets provide more screen area, for example, which means tablet users might spend more time browsing and reading text-based menus.
  • Location: Users in different locations may have access to a different number and variety of restaurants. Based on the restaurants available, the food delivery app may be more or less appealing to users from a certain location.
  1. Generate example segment divisions for each selected characteristic.
  • Age - Under 20, 21-25, 26-35, 36-45 and so on.
  • Device - Tablet vs. smartphone vs. desktop.
  • Location - Rural vs. suburban vs. urban. Or locations with a certain number of restaurants in the app per capita (for example, locations with less than 15 restaurants per capita, locations with 15-30 restaurants per capita, and locations with more than 30 restaurants per capita).
