04. Segment User Data

Segment User Data

Define Segment

ND030 C3 L3 04 Segmenting Data By User Cohorts Video

Summary

Define user segments

A user segment is a group of users that share something in common. When thinking about how to segment that user data, we can consider three main categories.

  • By user acquisition date (cohort analysis). This refers to when the user starts using a product or a feature.

  • By **demographic **similarities. Demographics are anything that describes a user as a person, such as income, age, and gender.

  • By user’s behavior when using a product or feature. This means they ways users use a product such as how often a product is visited or the device used.

Cohort analysis

A cohort is a group of people who start using the same product around the same time. Note: when segmenting users into cohorts, you should consider the pace at which changes are made in your product (by day, by week or by month, etc).

Product managers can use a cohort analysis to isolate the effect of product modification on user behavior. By noting the differences among cohorts, product managers can also measure the effect of cumulative changes over time.

Demographic and Behavior Segments

ND030 C3 L3 06 Demographic & Behavior Segments

Demographic segments

Demographic segments group users that share the same personal characteristics

Customers with demographics might have different needs that impact the way they use a product. Product managers use demographic segmentation to understand if personal characteristics result in different product experiences.

When significant differences among different groups occur, product managers can form a hypothesis for the cause of these results and then investigate further. Such investigations often lead to discoveries about the unmet needs of the underperforming segment. These unmet needs to become opportunities to boost KPIs with targeted product changes.

Behavior segments

Segmenting users based on behaviors means to group users who use a product (“behave”) in similar ways.

Different behaviors indicate that customers might have different motivations and needs. Segmenting users based on their behaviors allows product managers to find patterns about how our product is succeeding or failing specific types of users.

Data collection

Cohort analysis and behavioral information will come naturally from the instrumentation that has been put in place to track user actions.

To collect demographic information, however, you need to ask users to fill out profiles or answer questions about themselves.
A note of caution when seeking information on users:

  1. Make it clear to users why providing the information will be valuable to them.
  2. Keep in mind that consumer privacy laws such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) give users many rights with regards to their personal information.

New terms

  • Cohort: For analysis purposes, cohorts refer to groups of people who begin using a product or feature at the same time.
  • Cohort Analysis: A type of segment analysis that focuses specifically on user cohorts.
  • Demographics: Anything that describes a user as a person, such as income, age, and gender.
  • Segment Analysis: The process of analyzing how different groups of users compare in product or feature usage.
