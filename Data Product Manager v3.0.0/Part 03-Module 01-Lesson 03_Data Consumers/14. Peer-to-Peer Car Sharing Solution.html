Peer-to-Peer Car Sharing Solution

Important: Your thoughts may look different than this, and that is alright. Product Management is not conducted in a vacuum, and you will be working directly with the types of teams we are using in our example. Much of product management is subjective, but this course and it's exercises are meant to help you anticipate the needs of your stakeholders and help identify who you need to talk to.

Problem statement:

One example for our peer-to-peer car-sharing company could include the following business goals:

  • Risk and security team: focused on safety, and security of car owners and car renters
  • Accounting team: ensuring they are financially compliant
  • Payments Team: focused on smooth payment mechanisms for car owners and renters

Turo Use Cases

Peer-to-Peer Car Sharing Data Use Cases

To ensure safety and security, the risk and security team will be interested in customer and car owner screening.

The financial compliance accounting team will maintain financial books and create accurate balance sheets.

Managing seamless payments to car owners is a use case for the Payments team.

You can see potential Data Elements and the type of data in the table above. There could be others as well. For example, the payments team might want timestamps for payment events (credit card charge, credit card processing, payment sent, the payment received, payment verified) in order to monitor the performance of the payment process.

Entity Tables:

  • Car details
  • Booking details table

Below you can see how we might use our Entity Modeling for each use case.

Risk Image

risk and security

Risk and Security

For car owners screening, the risk and security team will combine the partner table with the partner payment table and car table. The information provided in these tables can be combined and used for partner verification.

Fraud algorithms can be built to detect anomalies. Similarly, customer screening can be done using the customer table's information, along with Facebook and Google login details. Again some ML modeling will be needed that will use these parameters to detect inconsistencies.

Finance Model

Accounting

Accounting

Accounting can calculate costs and margins using a booking table. From the booking total, daily rental cost, taxes, and fees would be subtracted to get the margin. All this will be aggregated on a monthly and weekly basis to make financial entries into books.

Payments Model

Payments

Payments

And finally, the payments team will combine data from the booking table, car table, partner table, and partner payment table to get details of what payment needs to be made and to whom. They will use the booking table to get what needs to be paid and figure out the margin.
